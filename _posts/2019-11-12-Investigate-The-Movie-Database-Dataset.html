Introduction¶

The Movie Database (TMDb) is a database of 10,000 movies, which has data about revenue, budget,cast,director,Production Comapny and the user ratings for each movie.

The final two columns ending with _adj show the budget and revenue of the associated movie in terms of 2010 dollars, accounting for inflation over time.

The questions that we can explore with this data are:

  • How have movie revenue changed over the years
  • What genres are most popular over the years
  • The relationship (correlation) between movie popularity,its rating and its vote count
  • How movie ratings affect revenue
  • Which movies have been the most profitable-some characteristics of these movies.

2. Revenue and Budget columns had some zero values, they needed to be dropped from analysis

A lot of values in the columns-budget, revenue, adj_budget and adj_revenue are zero . Since the budget and revenue of a movie cannot be zero therefore these are missing values. We cannot use mean revenue or mean budget to replace these zero values because this will distort the analysis.Hence for any analysis based on revenue, budget or profit of a movie, it is best to drop them and exclude from analysis.For this purpose I have created a separate variable 'valid_df' for analysing non zero revenue and budget movies.

# write a function to sort the movie genres

def movie_category_genres(data):
    """
        This function takes a Data Frame of movie categories and sorts their genres by the 
        number of times they appear in all the movies of that particular movie category
    """
    
    data_genre_list=[]
    
    for genre in data['genres']:
        for item in genre:
            data_genre_list.append(item)
            
    data_genre_set=set(data_genre_list)
    
    data_genre_tuple=[]
    for genre in data_genre_set:
        data_genre_tuple.append((genre,data_genre_list.count(genre)))
        
    return sorted(data_genre_tuple, key = lambda t:t[1], reverse=True)

Conclusions¶

  1. The revenue, budget and proft of the movie has increased over the years as number of movies that are made now are far greater in number than a few decades ago. This implies that the size of the industry has grown bigger over these years

  2. There seems to be a strong correlation between vote count and poularity. We do not know how popularity is calculated so we cannot say much about this variable except that a higher number denotes greater populairty. If a movie is higher on popularity score then its very likely to have a higher vote count ie more and more people vote for that movie.

  3. There also seems to be a very strong correlation between movie rating and the revnue. Higher rated movies earn more revenue and profit.

  4. The most popular genre in the highest rated movies is Documentary and Drama while Horror is the most popular genre in lowest rated movies. Action, Thriller and Comedy are popular with movie ratings in the middle

  5. The top 3 genres for the 100 most profitable movies is Action, Adventure and Fantasy

  6. The average revenue for the 100 most profitable movies was 1 billion while the average budget was 111 million.
